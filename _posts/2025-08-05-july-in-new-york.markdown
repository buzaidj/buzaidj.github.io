Earlier in the year I thought of moving to New York. My friend had an open room in her apartment and offered it to me. "$1100 a month and it's quiet," is what she said and it sounded like a dream. Something felt novel about New York. Even though I'd mostly be doing the same things in different place, I had the idea that a little refresh of setting would do wonders for wellbeing and productivity. And besides, my family is in New York, and spending more time with family felt right. I ultimately decided against the move. "Why change things if they are already pretty good?" was the question I couldn't answer. To compromise with my desire for a setting change I decided spending a month there would be about right. I'd spend some time at my Dad's in Westchester, some at my Mom's in Brooklyn, and some at a cheap sublet I found in Brooklyn.

My flight leaves today. The train ride to airport was pretty normal except for an unfortunate fare gate outage making the ride free. I am excited! It's a strange feeling considering all the thought and bargaining that went into the trip.
