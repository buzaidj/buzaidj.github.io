Earlier in the year I thought of moving to New York. My friend had an open room in her apartment and offered it to me. "$1100 a month and it's quiet," is what she said and it sounded like a dream. Something felt novel about New York. Even though I'd mostly be doing the same things in different place, I had the idea that a little refresh of setting would do wonders for wellbeing and productivity. And besides, my family is in New York, and spending more time with family felt right. I ultimately decided against the move. "Why change things if they are already pretty good?" was the question I couldn't answer. To compromise with my desire for a setting change I decided spending a month there would be about right. I'd spend some time at my Dad's in Westchester, some at my Mom's in Brooklyn, and some at a cheap sublet I found in Brooklyn.

Everyday city life was great. Compared to SF I felt like more of a real member of society for holing myself in an office away from the summer heat. I don't know why. Maybe it's the frequent and busy subways, or busy streets. I generally got off work around 5, or sometimes a little earlier, owing to that temporary decline in summer labor productivity. On overcast and rainy days without plans, I typically got on an uptown train to the New York Public Library Mid-Manhattan Branch with a JLPT N2 test prep book in hand. The library is big with a number of good study spaces, and is open till 9pm. The only annoyance are the distracting patrons doing things like gaming, taking phone calls, and playing tik-toks (just at a low enough volume out of the librarian's earshot).

Doing the things I already do and enjoy in a new setting give them a new novelty. I found cafes I liked, libraries I liked, new spots to read, and new bars to hang out with old friends in. The novelty would've faded in time, but I jumped back to SF life before I could really get used to it.
